Irfan Al-Hussaini

PhD Candidate

Georgia Institute of Technology

I am a PhD Candidate at Georgia Tech advised by Prof. Cassie Mitchell. My research focuses on interpretable machine learning for healthcare using domain knowledge:

  • document filtering, summarization, and entity recognition for synthesis of domain knowledge [SIGIR], [BioNLP, ACL], [BioNLP, ACL], [BigData]
  • optimizing features derived from domain knowledge to pair with glass-box models [ICASSP]
  • domain knowledge distillation applied to the interpretation of deep neural networks [CIKM], [TS4H, NeurIPS], [MLHC]

Previously, I worked on unsupervised learning during my internship at NVIDIA. I also contributed to the development of contact tracing graphs for COVID-19 during an summer internship at National Security Innovation Network, Department of Defense, and worked on a time-sensitive ethernet network project at the Georgia Tech Research Institute.

I was advised by Prof. Jimeng Sun for my MS thesis on interpretable machine learning for a clinical decision support system. During my undergrad at BUET, I worked on real-time music beat tracking.

I also regularly review for machine learning conferences, NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, BioNLP, ACL, and MLHC. Besides research, I am very interested in sports, particularly soccer and Arsenal FC.
